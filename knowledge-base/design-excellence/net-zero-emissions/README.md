# Net Zero Emissions & Sustainability Framework

**Authority:** NCC 2022 Energy Efficiency, NZEB (Net Zero Energy Building) Standards, Renewable Energy Integration  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-05

---

## Purpose

SDARPP facilities achieve Net Zero emissions (or better) — generating as much renewable energy as consumed annually, with carbon-negative design through sequestration.

---

## Net Zero Pathway

### Three Pillars

**1. Energy Reduction (Demand-Side)**
- High-efficiency HVAC systems (COP > 4.0)
- LED lighting throughout (< 3W per m²)
- Building envelope optimization (R-values exceed NCC minimums)
- Heat recovery systems (ERV/HRV)
- Demand-controlled ventilation (CO₂-based)
- Target: 50% energy reduction vs conventional

**2. Renewable Generation (Supply-Side)**
- Solar PV: 10-15 kW per 1000m² facility area
- Battery storage: 4-6 hour buffer capacity
- Wind (where applicable): evaluate small wind turbines
- Biogas (if waste treatment on-site)
- EV charging infrastructure (future-ready for transport)
- Target: 100%+ renewable energy generation

**3. Carbon Sequestration (Offset-Side)**
- Biophilic design with mature trees (carbon storage in biomass)
- Green roofs (carbon sequestration + thermal benefit)
- Natural materials (specify sustainably harvested timber — net carbon negative over building life)
- Soil carbon enhancement (landscaping practices)
- Target: Carbon-negative facility (sequester > emit)

---

## NCC 2022 Compliance

### Energy Efficiency Standards
- **Lighting** — max 6W/m² base lighting load
- **HVAC** — minimum COP 3.5 for cooling, 3.0 for heating
- **Envelope** — wall R≥2.0, roof R≥4.0, windows U<2.0
- **Water** — hot water system efficiency 65%+ for storage or 90%+ for instantaneous
- **Airtightness** — target ACH50 < 4 air changes per hour

### Energy Performance Rating (6-Star minimum)
- Modelling required during design
- Verification during construction
- Commissioning test on completion
- Annual reporting on performance

## System Integration

| System | Implementation | Benefit |
|--------|-----------------|---------|
| **Solar PV** | Rooftop + carpark canopies | Generates 80-90% annual consumption |
| **Battery Storage** | 100-200kWh (project-dependent) | Peak shaving, grid support, resilience |
| **Heat Pump HVAC** | Air-to-water or ground-coupled | >4.0 COP, eliminates gas |
| **Insulation** | Spray foam or mineral batts | Airtight envelope, eliminates thermal bridges |
| **LED Lighting** | Daylight sensors + dimming | <3W/m² load, human-centric circadian |
| **Water Efficiency** | Rainwater harvesting + greywater recycling | 50% water reduction |
| **EV Charging** | Level 2 chargers (7kW) at 10% parking | Future-ready for fleet electrification |

## Facility-Specific Adaptations

### NDIS SDA Housing
- **Rooftop solar:** Optimize for residential 24-hour load curve
- **Battery timing:** Discharge in evening peak (support participant comfort)
- **Heat pump:** Heating/cooling + domestic hot water
- **Rainwater:** Irrigation and toilet flushing

### Aged Care Facilities
- **Thermal comfort priority:** 24/7 HVAC with good zoning
- **Backup power:** Battery supports critical systems (lighting, refrigeration, call systems)
- **Waste management:** Composting reduces landfill, biogas potential

### Healthcare Outpatient/VMO
- **Peak management:** Battery reduces demand charge costs
- **Resilience:** 4-6 hour battery backup for critical equipment
- **Water:** Sterilization system efficiency

## Lifecycle Cost Analysis

### Capital Cost Increase: +5-12%
- Solar PV: $1.2-1.5/W (residential) to $0.8-1.0/W (utility scale)
- Battery: $100-150/kWh for modern LFP
- Heat pump: Similar to conventional HVAC
- Insulation upgrade: ~$2-5/m² additional cost
- **ROI:** 7-12 year payback through energy savings

### 30-Year Net Benefit: AUD $400K-800K+
- Annual energy savings: $15-25K
- Maintenance reduction: $5-10K annually
- Energy cost inflation hedge: 3-5% annually
- Potential revenue: solar export credits, grid services

## Monitoring & Verification

**Year 1 Commissioning:**
- System acceptance testing (PV output, HVAC efficiency, lighting)
- Energy baseline establishment
- Occupant training

**Ongoing (Annual):**
- Energy consumption vs. design prediction
- System performance metrics
- Identify degradation, optimization opportunities
- Report to stakeholders
