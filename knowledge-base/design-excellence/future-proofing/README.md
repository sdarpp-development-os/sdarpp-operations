# Future-Proofing & Resilience Framework

**Authority:** Climate Adaptation, Flexibility Design, Long-term Thinking (10-20 years)  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-05

---

## Core Principle

Design for flexibility, not predictions. Build systems that adapt as needs change, climate evolves, technology emerges.

---

## 10-20 Year Planning Horizons

### Near Term (2026-2030)
- **Climate:** Increased heat waves, extreme weather
- **Tech:** AI diagnostics, telemedicine, wearables
- **Demographic:** Aging population, NDIS growth
- **Design Response:** Passive survivability, AI infrastructure, flexible spaces

### Medium Term (2030-2035)
- **Climate:** Severe drought, extreme precipitation, ecosystem shifts
- **Tech:** Autonomous systems, real-time diagnostics, personalized medicine
- **Social:** Skills gaps, workforce changes
- **Design Response:** Water independence, modular tech, adaptive buildings

### Long Term (2035+)
- **Climate:** 2-3°C warming, migration pressure, resource scarcity
- **Tech:** Molecular diagnostics, autonomous care assistance
- **Economic:** Circular economy, service-based models
- **Design Response:** Zero-waste operations, highly flexible infrastructure

## Climate Adaptation Strategies

### Passive Survivability
- Building functions without HVAC for 7+ days
- Natural ventilation capability
- Thermal mass for temperature buffering
- Shading strategies for extreme heat

### Water Independence
- Rainwater capture (100 year, 24-hour storage)
- Greywater recycling (toilet, irrigation)
- Drought-resistant landscaping
- Water-efficient fixtures

### Extreme Weather Resilience
- Flood design (elevation, floodable zones, pumping)
- Wind-resistant structure (190+ km/h design)
- Fire-resistant materials (where applicable)
- Emergency power (battery backup 4-7 days)

### Workforce Adaptability
- Cross-training protocols (not specialized skills)
- Technology-agnostic operations (not dependent on one system)
- Supply chain diversification (not single-source critical)

## Modularity & Flexibility

### Structural
- **Open floor plans:** Reconfigure for changing uses
- **Removable partitions:** Adapt space without renovation
- **Loose-fit spaces:** Not built for single purpose
- **Overbuilt utilities:** 2x capacity for future additions

### Operational
- **Plug-and-play systems:** Add technology without disruption
- **Scalable staffing:** Design supports variable team sizes
- **Adaptive programs:** Flexible scheduling, programming
- **Open data:** Systems compatible with future solutions

### Financial
- **Lifecycle costing:** Design for 30+ year value
- **Modular capital:** Staged investment, not all upfront
- **Revenue flexibility:** Multiple use cases, income streams
- **Buyback/reuse:** Design for component recovery

---

## Pandemic Resilience

### Health Facility Lessons
- **Isolation capability:** Flexible quarantine spaces
- **Ventilation:** HEPA-grade air handling throughout
- **Staffing:** Cross-trained teams, supply chain redundancy
- **Supply chain:** 3-6 month emergency stocks
- **Communication:** Redundant systems (phone, internet, radio)

---

## Adaptive Reuse Capability

Design assuming facility may be repurposed:
- **Structural:** Clear spans, flexible utilities
- **Utilities:** Distributed systems, not centralized
- **Systems:** Standard protocols, not proprietary
- **Legal:** Minimal restrictions, flexible use
