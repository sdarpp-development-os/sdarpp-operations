# Aboriginal Design with Country Framework

**Authority:** Reconciliation Act 2023, Aboriginal cultural protocols, FPIC (Free Prior Informed Consent)  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-05

---

## Purpose

Aboriginal design honours Country, integrates traditional custodian knowledge, and demonstrates leadership in Reconciliation. For SDARPP facilities, Aboriginal design is not aesthetic add-on — it's structural integration of cultural stewardship, land respect, and Indigenous leadership in facility development.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Country Recognition
- **Acknowledge traditional custodians** — identify who cares for the land
- **Songlines & stories** — understand Country's narrative and significance
- **Seasons & cycles** — integrate traditional seasonal calendar
- **Sacred/culturally significant sites** — respect restrictions and protocols
- **Consultation mandatory** — FPIC required before design finalisation

### 2. Cultural Protocol Compliance
- **FPIC process** — Free Prior Informed Consent from traditional custodians BEFORE design
- **Co-design opportunity** — offer genuine partnership, not tokenistic consultation
- **Decision-making authority** — traditional custodians retain right to say "No"
- **Benefit-sharing** — identify how project benefits Indigenous community
- **Intellectual property** — protect cultural designs from misappropriation

### 3. Land Stewardship Integration
- **Sustainable land use** — design supports ecological health, not extraction
- **Water management** — traditional water practices honoured where applicable
- **Fire management** — cultural burning (if appropriate) integrated
- **Biodiversity enhancement** — design increases habitat for native species
- **Carbon sequestration** — contribute to climate solutions

### 4. Material Authenticity
- **Sourcing from traditional lands** — where possible & culturally appropriate
- **Certified Indigenous suppliers** — support Aboriginal-owned businesses
- **Cultural material use** — timber, stone, clay from traditional Country
- **Avoid cultural appropriation** — use materials appropriately, don't overuse sacred patterns

### 5. Design Integration (not Tokenism)
- **Structural, not surface** — Aboriginal design in fundamental architecture, not just artwork
- **Wayfinding systems** — use cultural narratives for navigation
- **Spatial organization** — reflect cultural understanding of space and community
- **Art integration** — commission Aboriginal artists for meaningful work
- **Naming systems** — use language names from traditional custodians

## Three Tiers of Aboriginal Design Engagement

### Tier 1: Acknowledgment (Minimum)
- Land acknowledgment statement
- Art by Aboriginal artists
- Collaboration with local Aboriginal organisations
- Recognition signage

### Tier 2: Integration (Better)
- Co-design process with traditional custodians
- Cultural narratives in wayfinding, spatial planning
- Indigenous procurement (>15% spending)
- Staff training on cultural protocols
- Regular community engagement

### Tier 3: Leadership (Best Practice)
- Joint governance model with traditional custodians
- Aboriginal staff in design & management roles
- Cultural Centre or learning space in facility
- Economic benefit-sharing model
- Ongoing cultural renewal and evolution

## Facility-Specific Integration

### NDIS SDA Housing
- **Participant identity support** — design celebrates cultural identity of residents
- **Family gathering spaces** — accommodate extended family visiting (cultural norms)
- **Country connection** — views/access to landscapes meaningful to residents
- **Cultural practices supported** — space for ceremonies, practices where appropriate

### Aged Care Facilities
- **Elder wisdom spaces** — designated areas for traditional knowledge sharing
- **Intergenerational design** — spaces for youth to learn from elders
- **Customary practices** — design accommodates cultural end-of-life practices
- **Country connection** — therapeutic value of cultural landscape connection

### Healthcare Facilities
- **Healing garden** — based on traditional medicine plants & practices
- **Cultural consultation rooms** — space for family and cultural support during treatment
- **Wayfinding** — narrative-based navigation using cultural stories
- **Artwork** — commissioned from regional Aboriginal artists

## Consultation Framework (FPIC Process)

**Phase 1: Identification** (Week 1-2)
- Identify traditional custodian group(s) for the land
- Research which communities connect to site
- Initial contact through established protocols

**Phase 2: Engagement** (Week 3-6)
- Present project to traditional custodians
- Explain full scope (design, operations, benefits)
- Offer meaningful participation opportunity
- Clarify: their role, decision-making authority, compensation

**Phase 3: Co-Design** (Week 7-14)
- Joint workshops with custodian representatives & designers
- Explore cultural integration opportunities
- Agree on naming, cultural elements, protocols
- Document agreed outcomes

**Phase 4: Implementation** (Ongoing)
- Honour agreed cultural elements
- Recognize custodians in acknowledgments
- Implement benefit-sharing arrangements
- Maintain ongoing relationship (not one-off consultation)

**Phase 5: Evaluation** (Annual)
- Assess cultural appropriateness
- Gather community feedback
- Update practices based on feedback
- Renew commitment to partnership

## Reconciliation Act Alignment

### Section 6 — Substantive Reconciliation
SDARPP facilities demonstrate:
- ✅ Genuine partnership with Indigenous peoples
- ✅ Economic benefit to Aboriginal communities
- ✅ Voice and decision-making authority for custodians
- ✅ Long-term commitment (not extractive)

### Section 8 — Truth-telling
- ✅ Acknowledge history of land & dispossession
- ✅ Honour ongoing custodianship
- ✅ Support Aboriginal self-determination

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Solution |
|-----------|-----------|
| Generic "Aboriginal art" from unrelated regions | Commission work from traditional custodians of YOUR land |
| Consultation after design finalised | FPIC BEFORE design (not adding art to finished design) |
| Aboriginal stakeholder without decision authority | Genuine partnership with decision-making power |
| One-off consultation, then silence | Ongoing relationship, annual review, evolution |
| Use of sacred patterns/stories without permission | Ask permission, compensate, use appropriately |
| Benefit extraction (taking cultural knowledge, no return) | Equitable benefit-sharing, economic support |

## Economic Integration

### Procurement
- Target 15-25% Aboriginal-owned supplier spending
- Support Aboriginal construction firms, designers, artists
- Training & employment for Aboriginal community members

### Operations
- Hire Aboriginal staff at all levels
- Leadership roles with decision authority
- Cultural consultation fees paid appropriately

### Ongoing
- Annual payment to custodian community (land stewardship fee)
- Support for cultural programs, events, practices
- Co-governance of cultural/heritage aspects
