# Staff Wellbeing Design Framework

**Authority:** Occupational Health & Safety, Burnout Prevention Research, Workplace Mental Health  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-05

---

## Principle

Burnout is a design problem, not a personality problem. Architecture and operations prevent exhaustion before it happens.

---

## Evidence-Based Interventions

### Physical Design for Mental Health

| Design Element | Impact | Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| **Natural light** | 15-20% lower burnout | Windows, skylights for all work areas |
| **Restorative spaces** | Recovery capacity | Quiet zones, gardens for breaks |
| **Ergonomic furniture** | Pain reduction, focus | Quality seating, height-adjustable desks |
| **Biophilic elements** | Stress reduction | Plants, views, natural materials |
| **Social spaces** | Connection, support | Lounge areas, dining, gathering |

### Operational Design

- **Rostering:** Predictable schedules, not last-minute changes
- **Staffing ratios:** Safe, sustainable levels (not crisis-mode)
- **Training:** Continuous development, skill building
- **Recognition:** Celebrate wins, acknowledge effort
- **Support:** Mental health resources, counseling, peer support

## Specific Spaces

### Staff Room / Lounges
- **Quiet retreat:** Separation from work environment
- **Restoration:** Natural light, biophilic elements
- **Comfort:** Quality furniture, rest pods available
- **Social:** Small tables for connection, not institutional
- **Respite:** Can be used for 5-15 min breaks

### Break Room / Kitchen
- **Quality food:** Options beyond vending machines
- **Dining quality:** Table seating, not standing/rushed
- **Social:** Intentional gathering space
- **Comfort:** Temperature, lighting suitable for relaxation

### Mental Health / Wellness Space
- **Confidentiality:** Private rooms for counseling
- **Calm:** Therapeutic design, natural elements
- **Accessibility:** Without stigma, normalized usage
- **Resources:** Information, support contacts, materials

### On-Site Accommodation (If Provided)
- **Hotel quality:** Not institutional housing
- **Separation:** Boundary between work/personal
- **Amenities:** Comfortable furnishings, privacy
- **Accessible:** Kitchen, laundry, entertainment

### Staff Development Space
- **Learning environment:** Bright, inspiring
- **Technology:** Modern training facilities
- **Flexible:** Adapts to different training formats
- **Accessible:** Welcoming for all learning styles

## Support Services

### Wellness Offerings
- **Mental health counseling** — EAP (Employee Assistance Program)
- **Fitness programs** — On-site gym or subsidized membership
- **Stress management** — Yoga, meditation, mindfulness
- **Professional development** — Training, certification support
- **Financial support** — Counseling, education benefits

### Work-Life Balance
- **Flexible scheduling** — Options for various needs
- **Remote work** — Where appropriate for role
- **Parental support** — Child care, flexible hours
- **Leave policies** — Generous, encouraged use
- **Career progression** — Pathways, mentoring

---

## Burnout Prevention Metrics

### Monitor & Respond

| Indicator | Frequency | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Staff turnover** | Quarterly | Track, investigate departures |
| **Wellness surveys** | Semi-annual | Anonymous, actionable feedback |
| **Sick leave usage** | Monthly | Identify patterns, support teams |
| **Incident rates** | Quarterly | Safety issues indicate fatigue |
| **Staff feedback** | Ongoing | Regular check-ins with managers |

### Targets
- Turnover <15% annually
- Sick leave <6 days/FTE annually
- Wellness survey >7/10 satisfaction
- Zero significant safety incidents
