# Medical Innovation & Future Tech Framework

**Authority:** Emerging Medical Technology Roadmap 2026+, Telemedicine Standards, AI Diagnostic Integration  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-05

---

## Core Principle: Design for Future Technology, Not Today's

Facilities must accommodate tech innovations not yet invented. Design flexibility > fixed infrastructure.

---

## Technology Readiness Matrix

### Telemedicine Infrastructure (Current)
- **Broadband:** Minimum 100Mbps download (gigabit preferred)
- **WiFi 6:** Throughout facility, redundant networks
- **Patient-facing:** Tablets/screens in treatment rooms, accessible
- **Staff devices:** Smartphones, laptops, wearables integrated
- **Privacy:** HIPAA-grade encryption, isolated networks

### AI Diagnostic Readiness (2026-2030)
- **Imaging specs:** Rooms pre-wired for AI analysis (structured data export)
- **Pathology integration:** Lab systems AI-compatible
- **Data architecture:** Standardized EHR formats (HL7 FHIR)
- **Computing capacity:** Edge computing on-site for real-time analysis
- **Staff preparation:** Training in AI-assisted diagnosis, human oversight

### Connected Health Systems (2026+)
- **Wearable sensors:** Participant monitoring (heart rate, O₂, falls)
- **Predictive analytics:** Early warning systems for deterioration
- **Mobile first:** Apps for participant engagement, feedback
- **Integration:** Seamless data flow between systems

### Robotics Integration (2028+)
- **Space planning:** Service robot corridors, charging stations
- **Structural:** Load-bearing floors for surgical robots if needed
- **Surgery:** Pre-designed OR for robotic surgery (optional add-on)
- **Logistics:** Autonomous delivery systems for supplies

## 10-20 Year Technology Roadmap

| Timeframe | Innovation | Design Implication |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Now (2026)** | Telemedicine, basic AI analysis | Broadband, data architecture |
| **Near (2028)** | Predictive health monitoring, robotics | Sensor infrastructure, flexible routing |
| **Medium (2030)** | Autonomous patient engagement bots, advanced diagnostics | AI-ready spaces, redundant networks |
| **Long (2035+)** | Molecular diagnostics, personalized medicine | Data center capacity, research spaces |

## Flexibility & Modularity

### Design Principles
- **Plug-and-play:** Add technology without renovating
- **Overbuilt capacity:** Install 2x required infrastructure (future-proof)
- **Reconfigurable space:** Treatment rooms adaptable for new modalities
- **Futureproof mechanical:** HVAC, power, water can be expanded
- **Emerging tech zones:** Dedicated space for pilots, testing

---

## Specific Facility Adaptations

### VMO Clinics
- **Telemedicine-first:** Dedicated rooms with high-quality video/audio
- **Remote diagnostics:** Integration with specialist AI analysis
- **Mobile records:** Seamless transition to telehealth-primary model

### Hospital Outpatient
- **AI imaging:** Radiology, pathology AI-compatible systems
- **Predictive:** Patient risk stratification before visit
- **Connected:** IoT devices throughout facility

### SDA Housing
- **Ambient monitoring:** Non-intrusive sensors for participant wellbeing
- **Emergency detection:** Falls, vital sign changes trigger immediate response
- **Engagement:** Telehealth for specialist consultations

### Aged Care
- **Predictive health:** Early intervention for decline
- **Social robots:** Companionship, activity engagement
- **Preventive:** Wearables detect health changes early
