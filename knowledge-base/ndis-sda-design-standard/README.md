# NDIS SDA Design Standard 2021

## Overview

The NDIS Specialist Disability Accommodation (SDA) Design Standard 2021 sets out the design and construction requirements for specialist disability accommodation funded through the NDIS.

The standard applies to new buildings and significant renovations that will be used as SDA under the NDIS.

## Three SDA Categories

### 1. Highly Accessible (HPS)
For people with physical disabilities requiring accessibility features.

**Key requirements:**
- Level access throughout (max 1:20 gradient ramps)
- Wide doorways (min 850mm clear opening)
- Accessible bathrooms and kitchens
- Accessible bedrooms with space for mobility equipment
- Adequate parking with accessible spaces

### 2. Fully Accessible (FA)
For people with severe physical and cognitive disabilities.

**Key requirements:**
- All HPS requirements, plus:
- Wider doorways (min 900mm)
- Enhanced bathroom and kitchen accessibility
- Specialised safety and supervision features
- Specific room sizes and configurations

### 3. Improved Liveability (IL)
For people with cognitive and developmental disabilities.

**Key requirements:**
- Enhanced cognitive accessibility
- Clear wayfinding and visual communication
- Reduced sensory overload considerations
- Safe outdoor spaces
- Community integration features

## Design Compliance Checklist

Each project must verify compliance across:
- Accessibility and mobility
- Bathrooms and wet areas
- Kitchens and food preparation
- Bedrooms and sleeping areas
- Communal and social spaces
- Outdoor areas and gardens
- Safety and emergency procedures
- Supervision and support spaces

## Australian Building Code (NCC) Integration

SDA design must also comply with the National Construction Code (NCC), specifically:
- **Class 3** buildings (multi-dwelling residential)
- **Class 9c** buildings (residential aged care)
- Accessibility standards (AS 1428 series)
- Building performance standards
